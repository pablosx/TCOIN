TCOIN Roadmap

This roadmap describes intent, not promises.

TCOIN evolves cautiously. Backward compatibility and honesty matter more than speed.


---

v0.1 — Genesis (Current)

Status: Released (v0.1-genesis)

Features:

Fixed max supply (15,000,000)

Human-readable JSON ledger

Phone-to-phone nodes (IP-based)

Manual peer discovery

Wallets with public/private keys

Explicit Developer Wallet recovery authority

Transparent recovery & correction transactions


Goals:

Prove the model works

Protect early users

Establish canonical rules



---

v0.2 — Trust Minimization

Status: Planned

Planned changes:

Mandatory transaction signatures

Balance verification on every sync

Ledger hash chaining

Reduced developer recovery scope

Improved peer conflict resolution


Non-goals:

Global scaling

Mining

Token incentives



---

v0.3 — Federation

Status: Concept

Potential features:

Multiple trusted issuers

Signed backing declarations

Optional federation bridges

Read-only relay nodes


Forks may experiment here.


---

v0.4 — Offline-First

Status: Research

Exploration areas:

QR-based transaction exchange

Delayed sync reconciliation

Portable ledger snapshots

Disaster-mode operation



---

v0.5 — Compute & Services

Status: Exploratory

Possible additions:

Paying for AI inference with TCOIN

Compute-time backing

Local service markets


This layer remains optional.


---

What TCOIN Will NOT Become

TCOIN explicitly rejects:

Proof-of-work mining

Proof-of-stake plutocracy

Hidden governance tokens

Forced pegs or algorithmic money



---

Canonical Stability

Breaking changes:

Require a new major version

Must be clearly tagged

Must not rewrite history


See CANONICAL.md.


---

Final Principle

> Stability is a feature. Power must move slowly.



TCOIN evolves by consent, not hype.
